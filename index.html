01 / About

Who am I?

I'm an electronics engineer turned space nerd -- cosmic dust about to collapse into a star! I'm a 3rd year PhD student at the Institute of Astronomy, University of Cambridge, funded by the Cambridge International & Isaac Newton Studentship. I work at the intersection of radio astronomy, machine learning, Bayesian statistics, and quantum computing to understand how the first stars and galaxies lit up the Universe during the Cosmic Dawn and Epoch of Reionization.

My research portfolio is wonderfully diverse: from hunting numerical instabilities in the REACH calibration pipeline, to modeling X-ray binary populations for 21-cm simulations, to simulating Fuzzy Dark Matter solitons on quantum computers, to probing exotic 21-cm signals with next-generation experiments. When I'm not chasing photons from the early Universe, you'll find me singing, experimenting in the kitchen, or stargazing under the Cambridge sky.

03 / Research

Research Work

REACH Calibration Numerical Stability Bayesian Inference

Numerical Instabilities in the REACH Calibration Pipeline

PhD Research -- University of Cambridge | Supervisor: Dr. Eloy de Lera Acedo

Investigating and correcting numerical instabilities in the calibration pipeline for the Radio Experiment for the Analysis of Cosmic Hydrogen (REACH) radiometer. This involves identifying failure modes in the Bayesian calibration framework, diagnosing their origins, and developing robust corrections to ensure reliable absolute calibration of the instrument for precision global 21-cm signal recovery.

X-ray Binaries Statistical Modeling 21-cm Simulations

X-ray Binary Luminosity Distributions & 21-cm Simulations

PhD Research -- University of Cambridge | Supervisors: Prof. Anastasia Fialkov & Dr. Boyuan Liu

Investigating the distribution of X-ray luminosities in X-ray binaries (XRBs) through innovative statistical modeling to understand the stochastic nature of XRB populations. Applying these insights to enhance 21-cm line simulations pivotal to the study of the Cosmic Dawn and Epoch of Reionization.

Quantum Computing Fuzzy Dark Matter IQAE Qiskit

Quantum Simulation of Fuzzy Dark Matter Solitons

PhD Research -- University of Cambridge

Simulating Fuzzy Dark Matter (FDM) soliton evolution using hybrid quantum-classical algorithms. Implementing Trotter-decomposed quantum circuits for the Schrödinger-Poisson system and using Iterative Quantum Amplitude Estimation (IQAE) for efficient density readout -- achieving quadratic speedup over direct sampling. This is the first demonstration of IQAE for density extraction in a quantum cosmological simulation, directly addressing the hybrid Poisson measurement bottleneck.

Exotic 21-cm Signals DEX ALO Detectability

Exotic 21-cm Signals & Detectability with DEX/ALO

PhD Research -- University of Cambridge

Exploring exotic 21-cm signal scenarios arising from non-standard physics and assessing their detectability with the Discovering the Epoch of Reionization's EXotic signatures (DEX) and Astronomy from the Lunar Orbit (ALO) experiments. This work pushes 21-cm cosmology beyond standard astrophysical models into the realm of new physics.

Line Intensity Mapping CII CO 21-cm Code

Line Intensity Mapping with CII/CO in 21-cm Simulations

Collaborative Project

Implementing Line Intensity Mapping (LIM) capabilities for CII and CO emission lines within a standard 21-cm simulation codebase. This multi-tracer approach enables cross-correlation studies between different emission lines and the 21-cm signal, opening new windows into the high-redshift Universe.

21-cm Cosmology SKA Machine Learning Bayesian Inference

ML-assisted Bayesian Pipeline for CD-EoR 21-cm Images

MS Thesis -- IIT Indore | Supervisors: Dr. Suman Majumdar & Prof. Abhirup Datta

Developed a Machine Learning-assisted Bayesian interpretation pipeline for Cosmic Dawn and Epoch of Reionization 21-cm tomographic images (to be produced by the SKA) using Largest Cluster Statistics (LCS) to understand the topological and morphological evolution of ionized hydrogen regions and draw inferences on ionizing sources and Inter-Galactic Medium properties.

SKA Data Challenge Radio Astronomy

SKA Science Data Challenge 3

SKA Science Working Group

Preparing the radio-astronomical community for next-generation data from the Square Kilometre Array. Working with realistic synthetic datasets simulating telescope capabilities to develop new analytical techniques for the order-of-magnitude increase in sensitivity.

Deep Learning CNN 21-cm

Neutral Fraction Prediction from HI 21-cm Images

Astro Hack Week 2022 -- Max Planck Institute for Astronomy

Developed a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) to predict the neutral fraction (xHI) directly from HI 21-cm images, providing a fast and accurate alternative to traditional analysis methods.

Digital Michelson Interferometer

Arduino DUE-based digital interferometer using LDRs to measure thin silver coatings on glass slabs. Measured thickness of 0.174 μm vs. reported 0.25 μm.
